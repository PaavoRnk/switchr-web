Common questions

Straight answers to
the obvious questions.

If you are hesitating, it is probably one of these. We would rather answer them upfront than have you wonder.

Never. Switching provider in Spain is a purely administrative process. Your physical electricity supply comes from the national grid (Red Eléctrica) and is completely unaffected. The switch simply changes who sends you a bill. Most customers don't notice anything until a cheaper invoice arrives.
Your new provider needs your IBAN to set up the direct debit for your monthly bills — exactly the same arrangement you have with your current provider right now. Switchr never charges you anything and does not store your full IBAN after it is submitted to the provider.
Nothing. Switchr is completely free for customers. We earn a commission from the provider when you switch — similar to how a mortgage broker or insurance comparison site works. This commission has no effect on the tariff price you pay.
You can cancel within your first year with no penalty. Just contact your new provider and they will process it. You can switch to another provider at any time — including back to your previous one if you prefer.
Filling in the form takes about 2 minutes. Once submitted, your new provider notifies your current supplier within 24 hours. The technical switch completes in 3–4 working days with no interruption. Your first bill from the new provider usually arrives 30–40 days later.
