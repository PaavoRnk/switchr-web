Your electricity bill probably has charges on it you never agreed to

27 April 2026 · 4 min read · electricity bills add-ons Iberdrola expat guide Spain

Pull out your last Spanish electricity bill. Find the section labelled "Servicios y Otros Conceptos," or sometimes just "Otros Servicios." It might be near the bottom, easy to skip past.

If there's anything listed there, read it carefully.

Those line items are not electricity charges. They're add-on products: home insurance, payment protection, technical assistance services, appliance cover. And in a significant number of cases, the people paying for them have no memory of signing up.

This isn't a small issue. In the bills we process at Switchr, over half of Iberdrola customers have at least one of these extras. The monthly costs range from €1.95 to €11 per service, and some bills carry two or three of them. That adds up to €100 or more per year on things that have nothing to do with powering your home.

How do they get there?

Usually one of three ways.

The most common is that they were included in your original contract and you didn't notice. Electricity contracts in Spain are long, dense documents in Spanish, and the add-ons are typically buried in supplementary terms rather than highlighted upfront.

The second is that a customer service call at some point included an upsell that wasn't clearly explained. "We can add home emergency cover for just a small monthly fee" sounds reasonable in the moment. Less so when you find it's been running for three years.

The third is inheritance: if you took over a contract from a previous tenant or owner, you may have taken on their add-ons too without realising it.

The most common ones to look for

On Iberdrola bills specifically, you'll often see variations of these:

  • "Protección Eléctrica Hogar" or "Protección Eléctrica Hogar Plus" is an electrical installation insurance product. Monthly cost typically €3–9.
  • "Seguro Protección de Pagos" is a payment protection insurance. Monthly cost typically €2–3.
  • "Asistente Smart" or "Asistente Smart Hogar" is a home assistance service. Monthly cost typically €3.
  • "Pack Iberdrola Hogar" is a bundled cover product. Variable cost.

Other providers have their own versions. Endesa has "Seguros Endesa," Repsol bundles a "Tu Asistente 24h." The names change but the pattern is the same.

What can you actually do about it?

If you find something you don't want, you can cancel these add-ons independently of your electricity contract. Call your provider's customer service line and ask to remove the service by name. In Spain you have the right to cancel additional services without affecting your main supply contract.

It takes a phone call. Not a pleasant one necessarily, but a straightforward one.

The bigger picture

The add-ons are frustrating, but they're often not the main reason people overpay. The tariff itself is usually where the larger saving sits.
